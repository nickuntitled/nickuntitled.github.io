Newspaper - When I read less

May 29, 2015

Reading time ~1 minute

Newspaper, one of mass media printed on low-grade paper, consists of news, informative articles and advertising. The news includes crimes, politics, economics, health, technology, sports, entertainment etc.

In Thailand, newspaper is one of the most popular mass media printed on A3 paper. There are many publishers such as Thairath, Dailynews, Khaosod, The Nation, Bangkok Post etc. Thairath is newspaper I read everyday. However, I have read newspaper less for approximately six months due to overwhelmed bad news and ridiculous news.

First, there are too many bad news. When I receive Thai newspaper every morning at 6:00 am, I read a first page which has many headlines. Almost every news shown on first page mainly forcus on politics, accidents, crimes or religions. There are few good news shown on first page such as winning Olympic prize or winning sports. After I read on the websites which I forgot, I found one reason many publishers write bad news. It is audience. The more bad news they publish, the more audience they have.

The second reason is ridiculous. There are too many news I consider it is ridiculous. When I read Thairath or Matichon, I found every newspaper showed headlines about a girl posted video on YouTube about something stolen. Then, tomorrow, an authority said they would find who stole. I thought publishers should not pay attention to news like that.

According to two reasons above, I consider to read news from the internet instead of newspaper.

However, many news posted on the internet are fake. When I read, I try to search from Google to prove whether it is true or not. I think this is my best method to prevent spreading fake news to friends.
